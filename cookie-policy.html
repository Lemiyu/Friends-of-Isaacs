1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, or to work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site.

Cookies are not harmful and cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to your device. They are uniquely assigned to you and can only be read by a web server in the domain that issued the cookie.

2. How we use cookies

The Friends of Isaac's Food Bank CIC website is a static site with no login system, membership area, or e-commerce functionality. As a result, we use a minimal number of cookies.

We may use cookies to:

  • Ensure the website loads and functions correctly across your visit.
  • Understand, in aggregate and anonymous terms, how visitors use our site so we can improve it.

We do not use cookies to identify you personally, track you across other websites, or serve targeted advertising.

3. Types of cookie we use

Category Purpose Duration Required?
Strictly necessary Required for the website to function. These cannot be switched off. Session Yes
Analytics Helps us understand how visitors interact with the site in anonymous, aggregated form. No personal data is collected. Up to 2 years No — opt-in only

If we add any additional cookies in future — for example as part of a payment integration or donation system — this policy will be updated before those cookies are placed.

4. Third-party cookies

Our website contains links to third-party services including Facebook, GoFundMe, and Amazon. If you click through to any of these sites, they may set their own cookies on your device. We have no control over these cookies and recommend reading the privacy and cookie policies of those services directly.

We do not embed third-party tracking scripts, social media widgets, or advertising networks directly into our pages.

5. Managing and disabling cookies

You can control and manage cookies in several ways. Please note that removing or blocking cookies may affect your experience of our website.

Browser settings

Most browsers allow you to manage cookie preferences through their settings. You can typically:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually or in bulk.
  • Block third-party cookies.
  • Block all cookies from specific websites.
  • Block all cookies entirely.

For guidance on managing cookies in your specific browser, visit the browser's help section or one of the following:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Opt-out tools

For information about opting out of analytics cookies more broadly, you can visit Your Online Choices or install the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on if applicable.

6. Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time, for example if we add new functionality to the website. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated date at the top. We encourage you to check back periodically.
