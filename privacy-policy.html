Who we are

Friends of Isaac's Food Bank CIC is a Community Interest Company that provides food support and practical assistance to individuals and families experiencing hardship. We operate six collection sites, open 365 days a year, enabling people to access food parcels when they need them most.

We are committed to protecting your personal information and respecting your privacy in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Friends of Isaac's Food Bank CIC is the Data Controller responsible for your personal information.

  • Address: 51 Throckmorton Road, Redditch, B98 7RP
  • Email: friendsofisaacs@hotmail.com
  • Telephone: 07496 381323

What information we collect

The information we collect depends on your relationship with us.

Service users

We may collect:

  • Name, address, telephone number, and email address
  • Household composition
  • Referral information
  • Details relating to your support needs
  • Records of food parcels provided
  • Correspondence relating to support received

We do not routinely collect health or dietary information unless it is voluntarily disclosed and necessary to provide support.

Referrals

We accept both professional referrals and self-referrals. Where referrals are made by third parties, we may receive information from local authorities, schools, housing providers, social workers, support organisations, and other charities and community groups.

Volunteers

We may collect name, address, contact details, emergency contact information, volunteer availability, volunteer records and training information, and DBS information where required.

Donors and supporters

We may collect name, contact details, donation history, and Gift Aid information where applicable.

Website users

If you contact us through our website or by email, we may collect information you choose to provide.

Why we use your information

We use personal information to:

  • Provide food and support services
  • Manage food parcel requests and referrals
  • Communicate with service users
  • Recruit and manage volunteers
  • Process donations and Gift Aid claims
  • Respond to enquiries
  • Meet legal and regulatory requirements
  • Monitor and improve our services
  • Demonstrate impact to funders and stakeholders

Lawful basis for processing

We process personal information under one or more of the following lawful bases:

  • Legitimate interests — to provide food support services, manage referrals, communicate with service users, and operate our organisation effectively.
  • Contract — where processing is necessary to fulfil arrangements with volunteers, employees, contractors, or service providers.
  • Legal obligation — where we are required to process information to comply with legal or regulatory obligations.
  • Consent — where consent is required, such as for the use of photographs, testimonials, case studies, or certain marketing communications. You may withdraw your consent at any time.

Sharing your information

We may share information where necessary with:

  • Referral agencies
  • Local authorities
  • Partner organisations providing support services
  • Funding bodies where anonymised or aggregated reporting is required
  • Regulatory authorities where legally required

We will never sell your personal information to third parties. Information will only be shared where it is lawful, necessary, and proportionate to do so.

How long we keep your information

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected and to meet legal, regulatory, and operational requirements. Specific retention periods are detailed within our Data Retention Schedule.

Keeping your information safe

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal information, including:

  • Password-protected systems
  • Restricted access to records
  • Secure storage of paper records
  • Confidentiality obligations for staff and volunteers
  • Secure disposal of information when no longer required

Photographs, testimonials, and case studies

Friends of Isaac's Food Bank CIC may use photographs, testimonials, and case studies to promote our work, celebrate achievements, and demonstrate impact.

Where an individual can be identified, we will seek appropriate consent before publication unless another lawful basis applies. Consent may be withdrawn at any time by contacting us at friendsofisaacs@hotmail.com.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Be informed about how your information is used
  • Access your personal information
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of information in certain circumstances
  • Restrict processing in certain circumstances
  • Object to processing where applicable
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at friendsofisaacs@hotmail.com or on 07496 381323.

Complaints

If you have concerns about how we use your personal information, please contact us first so that we can try to resolve the matter.

You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK's independent authority for data protection:

  • Website: ico.org.uk
  • Telephone: 0303 123 1113

Changes to this Privacy Notice

We may update this Privacy Notice from time to time to reflect changes in legislation, guidance, or our organisational practices. The latest version will always be available on our website.
